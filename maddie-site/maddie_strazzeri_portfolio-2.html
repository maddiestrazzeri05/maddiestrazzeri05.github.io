Who I Am

Collaborative by nature.
Purposeful by practice.

I am a double-major in Business Marketing and Business Management and Leadership at Saint Mary's College of California, and I have spent my time here doing exactly what those two disciplines ask of you — learning how to reach people, and learning how to lead them somewhere worth going.

My Marketing major has sharpened the way I read an audience, build a message, and stay consistent across every touchpoint. My Management and Leadership major has shaped how I rally a team, navigate complexity, and follow a vision all the way through to completion. Together, they are the foundation for a career rooted in one belief: that understanding the person in front of you is the most important thing you can do in business.

That belief did not come from a textbook. It came from years of watching brands, organizations, and communities show up for the people they serve — and from building my own experiences around that same standard. Every event I have planned, every student I have mentored, and every campaign I have run has been shaped by the question: what does this person actually need, and how do we meet them there?

Event Strategy · Community Outreach · May 2025

Reviving a Tradition: The Speed & Style Car Show

As President of the Dante Club at Saint Mary's College of California, I led the full revival of the Speed and Style Car Show — bringing it back for the first time since 2019. From concept to completion, I designed the event's structure, managed logistics across Campus Safety, Dining, and Recreation, conducted targeted outreach to Bay Area car clubs and community members, and executed a comprehensive marketing strategy that drove attendance from across the region. The result was 40+ vehicles, food vendors, live music, lawn games, a wine garden, and a turnout that validated the car show as a lasting annual tradition. Admission and car entry were completely free — because the best events are the ones that belong to everyone.

How I Work

The beliefs behind
everything I do.

These are not talking points. They are the things I have learned by doing the work — on campus, in the community, and in every room where someone trusted me to show up.

01

The customer is always a person first.

Whether the customer is a student choosing a major, a vendor trusting you with their business's debut, or a car enthusiast driving three hours to an event you planned — they are not a demographic. They are a person. Every decision I make in marketing, in event planning, and in mentorship starts there.

02

Real connection requires real presence.

The best outreach I have ever done did not happen through a caption or a flyer. It happened in a conversation — with a campus department head, a local business owner, a Bay Area car club, a first-year student who did not know where to start. Showing up in person, with intention, is still the most powerful marketing tool there is.

03

Excitement for the product is non-negotiable.

I have never been able to promote something I did not believe in, and I have never had to. The events I plan, the brands I admire, and the organizations I represent are ones I would champion whether or not it was my job. That genuine care comes through in every email, every post, and every conversation — and it is the difference between messaging that lands and messaging that gets scrolled past.
